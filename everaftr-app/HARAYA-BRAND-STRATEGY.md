## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### What Is haraya?

haraya is the Philippines' first inclusive, culturally rooted celebration planning platform. The name comes from the Filipino/Tagalog word meaning "imagination," "creative vision," and "dream" -- a word that captures the emotional heart of what every couple does when they begin planning their future together. They dream. They imagine. They envision. haraya is where those dreams take shape.

The platform combines a vendor directory, planning tools, an AI assistant named Kasa, a matchmaker quiz, community features, real celebration stories, and couple websites -- all designed mobile-first for Filipino couples aged 25-35, inclusive of every ceremony type, every love story, and every budget.

### Why We Rebranded

The project began as "everaftr" with a Woven Heritage design system rooted in six Filipino textile traditions. While the cultural research was valuable, the brand had accumulated complexity: a textile-to-section color mapping that users would never perceive, a dropped-E naming convention that created friction in functional UI, and a visual language (warm ivories, heritage reds, woven borders) that, while culturally rich, felt more archival than aspirational. The platform needed a name and identity that Filipino couples would feel in their bones, not one they needed explained.

"haraya" achieves this. It is a single Filipino word that every Tagalog speaker recognizes. It is beautiful in speech and in writing. It immediately communicates what the platform is about -- dreams, imagination, creative vision -- without requiring a cultural footnote. The visual identity shifts from textile heritage to an ethereal, dreamy aesthetic built on Dream Lavender and Twilight Blue, which feels modern, inclusive, and unmistakably aspirational.

This is not a pivot. It is an evolution. The core strategic foundations remain: Filipino cultural intelligence as the deepest moat, inclusivity built into the architecture, vendor trust through ethical practices, and a mobile-first experience designed for the realities of the Philippine market. What changes is the name, the visual identity, the emotional register, and the brand personality. Everything gets lighter, dreamier, and more inviting.

### What This Means

- **For the product:** The codebase transitions from the everaftr/Woven Heritage design system to the haraya design system. All color tokens, typography, component styles, and brand copy must be updated.
- **For the team:** All internal documents, social handles, domain registrations, and communications reference "haraya" going forward.
- **For vendors:** Any vendor outreach, partnership materials, and listing pages carry the haraya brand.
- **For couples:** They will meet a platform that feels like a dream they are stepping into, not a heritage museum they are visiting.

## 2. BRAND IDENTITY

### 2.1 Brand Name

**haraya**

- Always written in lowercase: `haraya`
- Never capitalized: not "Haraya", "HARAYA", or "HaRaYa"
- One word, no spaces, no hyphens, no abbreviations
- Pronounced: hah-RAH-yah (three syllables, stress on the second)
- Origin: Filipino/Tagalog -- meaning imagination, creative vision, dream, the power of the mind to envision what does not yet exist

The name was chosen because:
1. It is a real Filipino word that Tagalog speakers recognize immediately
2. It captures the emotional truth of celebration planning: every couple starts by dreaming
3. It is beautiful in both speech and writing
4. It works in English and Filipino contexts without translation
5. It is distinctive, memorable, and available as a brand

### 2.2 Tagline

**Primary tagline:** "where dreams take shape"

This tagline works on two levels. Literally, haraya (the platform) is the place where the dreams of a celebration take tangible form through vendor discovery, planning tools, and community support. Metaphorically, it speaks to the universal human experience of turning an imagined future into a lived reality.

**Secondary taglines (use as appropriate):**
- "imagine your celebration" -- for onboarding, quiz entry points, aspirational moments
- "every dream, every celebration" -- for inclusive messaging, homepage hero alternatives
- "dream it. plan it. celebrate it." -- for action-oriented contexts, feature marketing
- "your celebration, beautifully imagined" -- for premium/aspirational contexts

**Retired taglines (do not use):**
- "plan together, celebrate forevr" (everaftr)
- "where forevr begins" (everaftr)
- "six threads. one platform. every couple." (everaftr/Woven Heritage)
- "for every couple. every love story. every celebration." (everaftr)

### 2.3 Brand Promise

haraya promises Filipino couples a celebration planning experience that honors their dreams, respects their identity, and makes the journey from imagination to reality feel beautiful rather than overwhelming.

### 2.4 Brand Personality

haraya's personality is defined by five adjectives:

1. **Dreamy** -- The brand lives in the space between imagination and reality. Everything about haraya feels aspirational and ethereal, like the best version of a couple's vision made tangible. This is not fantasy; it is grounded dreaming.

2. **Warm** -- haraya is never cold, clinical, or transactional. The platform speaks to couples like a trusted friend who genuinely cares about their celebration. The warmth comes from knowing Filipino culture, respecting every relationship, and celebrating the emotional weight of what couples are building.

3. **Inclusive** -- haraya sees every love story. Catholic, civil, Muslim, LGBTQ+, intercultural, destination, intimate, grand -- all celebrations are welcomed by default, not as a feature to be toggled on. Inclusivity is the architecture, not the marketing.

4. **Modern** -- haraya feels current, clean, and technologically confident. The design is contemporary without being cold. The tools are smart without being intimidating. The experience is what Filipino couples expect from a platform built in 2026, not 2001.

5. **Filipino** -- haraya is rooted in the Philippines. Not as a heritage exhibit, but as a living, breathing understanding of how Filipino couples actually plan celebrations. The platform knows about ninongs, Pre-Cana, CENOMAR, same-day edit videos, and the tita politics of guest list management. This cultural intelligence is woven into every feature and every piece of content.

### 2.5 Brand Voice Guidelines

haraya speaks with a consistent voice across all touchpoints: the product UI, social media, email communications, vendor outreach, and customer support.

**Tone:** Gentle, encouraging, and knowledgeable. haraya is the friend who has already planned a celebration and is now quietly, warmly helping you plan yours. Never pushy. Never salesy. Never condescending.

**Register:** Conversational but polished. haraya uses complete sentences but avoids corporate stiffness. The language is accessible to the 25-35 Filipino demographic without being overly casual or using excessive slang.

**Vocabulary Principles:**

| Principle | What This Means | Example |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| Dream language | Use words that evoke imagination, vision, aspiration | "Imagine your perfect venue" not "Search for venues" |
| Warm personalization | Address the couple directly, use "you" and "your" | "Your celebration is taking shape" not "The celebration plan has been updated" |
| Filipino naturalness | Use Filipino terms where they feel natural, not forced | "Confirm your ninong/ninang list" not "Confirm your sponsors list" |
| Gentle encouragement | Celebrate progress, never pressure | "Beautiful progress -- you have completed 12 tasks" not "You still have 32 tasks remaining!" |
| Inclusive defaults | Never assume gender, ceremony type, or relationship structure | "You and your partner" not "bride and groom" |

**Voice Examples:**

| Context | Do Write | Do Not Write |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Homepage hero | "Where dreams take shape. Plan your celebration with tools built for Filipino couples." | "Find the best wedding suppliers! Book now!" |
| Empty state (no vendors saved) | "Your favorites list is waiting. Browse vendors and save the ones that spark something." | "No favorites yet. Add vendors to your list." |
| Checklist completion | "Another step closer to your dream celebration." | "Task completed." |
| Quiz invitation | "What does your dream celebration look like? Let's find out together." | "Take our quiz to get matched with vendors!" |
| Error state | "Something went wrong on our end. We are working on it -- your planning data is safe." | "Error 500. Please try again later." |
| Vendor outreach | "We built haraya because Filipino couples deserve better planning tools. We would love to feature your work." | "List your business on our platform for free leads!" |

**Code-Switching (Taglish):** In social media content (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook), haraya uses natural Filipino-English code-switching that mirrors how the target audience actually speaks. In product UI and formal communications, English is the primary language with Filipino terms used where culturally appropriate (ninong/ninang, Pre-Cana, CENOMAR, etc.).

### 2.6 Core Values

1. **Every dream is valid.** A PHP 50,000 intimate civil ceremony is celebrated with the same respect as a PHP 3,000,000 grand cathedral wedding. haraya never creates hierarchies of celebration.

2. **Every love story belongs.** Inclusivity is not a feature -- it is the foundation. The platform is built from the database schema up to welcome every couple, every ceremony type, and every relationship.

3. **Filipino couples deserve world-class tools.** The Philippines has no dominant digital wedding platform. haraya exists to close that gap with a product that stands alongside the best planning platforms globally while being deeply rooted in Filipino reality.

4. **Vendors are partners, not products.** haraya will never use pay-to-play ranking, long-term contracts, or mass-inquiry spam. Vendors are treated as collaborators whose success is our success.

5. **Dreams need structure.** Imagination without tools is just wishing. haraya gives dreams a backbone: checklists, budgets, timelines, and vendor connections that turn vision into reality.

## 3. VISUAL IDENTITY SUMMARY

### 3.1 Design Philosophy

The haraya visual identity is built on the concept of **ethereal dreaming made tangible**. Where the previous Woven Heritage system drew from textile traditions and earthy, warm tones, the haraya system draws from the liminal space between sleep and waking -- the moment when a dream is vivid enough to feel real. The palette is soft, luminous, and slightly otherworldly. The typography balances elegance with readability. The signature elements (flowing infinity mark, dream gradients, shimmer effects) create a visual language that feels like stepping into a beautiful dream.

**Design Principles:**
- **Light over heavy.** Backgrounds are cloud-white and pearl, not ivory and cream. The overall impression is bright and airy.
- **Soft over sharp.** Borders are gentle, corners are rounded, shadows are diffused. Nothing feels harsh or angular.
- **Luminous over flat.** Subtle gradients, shimmer effects, and layered transparency create depth without heaviness.
- **Spacious over dense.** Generous whitespace lets the content breathe. The platform never feels cramped.
- **Dreamy over decorative.** Visual interest comes from color, light, and space -- not from patterns, textures, or ornamental elements.

#### Semantic Colors

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Success** | `#4A9D7E` | Completed items, confirmations, positive states |
| **Warning** | `#D4A843` | Pending items, caution states, approaching deadlines |
| **Error** | `#D45B5B` | Error messages, destructive actions, alerts |
| **Info** | `#5B7C99` (Twilight Blue) | Informational messages, tips, guidance |

#### Color Usage Rules

1. **Dream Lavender is the hero.** It appears in the logo, primary CTAs, active navigation states, and key brand moments. It should be immediately recognizable as "the haraya color."
2. **Twilight Blue grounds the experience.** Use it for secondary actions, informational elements, and anywhere the design needs to feel calm and trustworthy rather than whimsical.
3. **Accent colors are supporting players.** Sunset Blush, Golden Hour, and Morning Mist appear in badges, highlights, and decorative moments -- never as primary action colors.
4. **Canvas colors create the stage.** The background hierarchy is Cloud White (page) > Pearl (cards) > Whisper (inputs/sections). This subtle layering creates depth without borders.
5. **Gradients are signature.** The haraya brand uses soft gradients (Dream Lavender to Twilight Blue, Dream Lavender to Sunset Blush) as signature elements in headers, hero sections, and feature illustrations. Gradients should always feel like a soft wash, never a sharp transition.
6. **Dark mode:** Invert backgrounds to Midnight (#2A2838) with Dream Lavender and Twilight Blue accents maintained. Text becomes Pearl (#FDFCFE). Accent colors may need 10-15% brightness increase for contrast.

### 3.4 Signature Visual Elements

#### The Flowing Infinity Mark

The haraya logo features a flowing infinity mark that represents:
- The endless nature of love and dreams
- Two paths (two partners) flowing together
- Continuity -- from imagination through planning to celebration and beyond

The mark is rendered in Dream Lavender on light backgrounds and Pearl on dark backgrounds. It can appear alongside the wordmark or independently as an icon/favicon.

#### Dream Gradients

Soft, luminous gradients are a signature haraya visual element.

**Primary gradient:** Dream Lavender (#9D8BD8) to Twilight Blue (#5B7C99)
- Used in: Hero sections, page headers, feature illustrations, loading states

**Warm gradient:** Dream Lavender (#9D8BD8) to Sunset Blush (#F4B8C5)
- Used in: Celebration moments, love-related features, quiz results

**Neutral gradient:** Morning Mist (#C5D5E4) to Cloud White (#F7F5FB)
- Used in: Subtle backgrounds, card hover states, section transitions

Gradients should always feel like a soft wash of color, never a hard-edged stripe. Use radial or angled linear gradients (135 degrees is the standard angle) with generous spread.

## 5. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### 5.1 The Philippine Wedding Planning Landscape

The Philippine wedding industry serves approximately 370,000-415,000 couples annually, with total market value estimated between PHP 74 billion and PHP 207 billion. Despite this scale, there is no dominant digital planning platform. Couples cobble together their planning experience from Facebook groups, Instagram searches, word-of-mouth, and spreadsheets. This fragmentation is the opportunity.

### 5.2 Competitive Analysis

#### vs. Facebook Groups (Weddings PH, Budget Wedding Philippines, etc.)

**What they offer:** Zero-friction access, social proof from real people, massive reach, real-time responses.

**Where they fail:** Unsearchable (a post from 3 days ago is effectively lost), unstructured (vendor recs mix with rants and spam), no tools (no budget tracker, no checklist, no vendor comparison), no verification (anyone can recommend or trash a vendor), contradictory advice, no privacy.

**haraya's advantage:** haraya does not replace Facebook groups -- it gives couples the structured tools Facebook groups cannot provide. The positioning is not "leave your groups." It is "keep your groups for the chismis, use haraya for the actual planning." A couple can get a vendor name from a Facebook group, then look them up on haraya to see their full portfolio, pricing range, reviews, and availability -- and add them to their shortlist, their budget, and their checklist in one flow.

#### vs. Kasal.com

**What they offer:** Longevity (established 2001), wedding fair organization (Kasalang Filipino series), all-inclusive wedding packages, vendor directory, privilege card discounts.

**Where they fail:** Dated user experience that has not meaningfully evolved in years. Desktop-first design in a mobile-first market. Bride-focused language and imagery. No modern planning tools (no interactive checklist, no budget tracker). No vendor search/filter functionality comparable to modern platforms. Functions primarily as a wedding fair organizer with a web directory attached.

**haraya's advantage:** Modern, mobile-first UX designed for 2026. Interactive planning tools. Inclusive language and design from the ground up. AI-powered assistant (Kasa). Vendor profiles with portfolio galleries, pricing transparency, and structured inquiry forms. The experience gap between Kasal.com and haraya is generational.

#### vs. Bride and Breakfast

**What they offer:** Strong editorial content and brand (405K Facebook followers, 228K Instagram followers). Beautiful wedding stories and vendor features. Toast Wedding Fair events. Established SEO authority for Philippine wedding content.

**Where they fail:** Fundamentally a content/media company, not a planning platform. No vendor search, no filtering, no comparison tools. No planning features (checklists, budgets, timelines). No marketplace interaction (cannot send inquiries, save favorites, or track vendors). The name itself ("Bride") signals a gendered, bride-focused perspective that limits inclusivity.

**haraya's advantage:** haraya is a platform, not a publication. Couples can browse, search, filter, compare, save, inquire, plan, and track -- all in one place. Bride and Breakfast is a potential content partner, not a competitor. haraya should approach B&B as a complementary brand: they inspire, haraya executes.

#### vs. Brideworthy

**What they offer:** Vendor directory with category browsing (venues, photographers, florists, caterers, etc.). 360-degree venue views. Direct supplier communication (no commission model). Clean, modern-ish design relative to Kasal.com.

**Where they fail:** Pink-dominated aesthetic that signals a luxury, bride-focused brand -- not inclusive by default. "Bride Worthy" as a name explicitly centers brides, excluding partners, grooms, and non-binary individuals. No planning tools. Small team (1-10 employees per public data). Limited differentiation beyond being a directory. No community features, no AI, no interactive planning.

**haraya's advantage:** Inclusive from the name down -- haraya is a gender-neutral Filipino word that speaks to everyone. Planning tools create stickiness that a directory alone cannot achieve. AI assistant (Kasa) provides personalized guidance. Matchmaker Quiz drives viral acquisition. The visual identity (Dream Lavender, ethereal aesthetic) is distinctive and aspirational without being gendered.

### 5.3 What haraya Does That No One Else Does

These five capabilities create a competitive moat that is structurally difficult to replicate:

1. **Filipino Cultural Intelligence as a Product Feature.** The checklist includes CENOMAR applications and Pre-Cana scheduling. The budget tracker uses PHP with per-head catering categories. The AI assistant (Kasa) understands ninong/ninang dynamics, same-day edit video as a vendor category, and the difference between a Catholic ceremony timeline and a Muslim nikah. No international platform can replicate this depth. No existing Philippine platform has built it.

2. **Inclusive Architecture, Not Inclusive Marketing.** The database schema uses `partner1_name` and `partner2_name`. The ceremony type enum includes `civil_union`. The default UI says "You" and "Your Partner" -- warm and personal for everyone. The "All Celebrations Welcome" badge is built into the vendor model. This is not a marketing stance; it is the engineering.

Kasa provides personalized vendor recommendations, budget advice, and answers to the specific questions Filipino couples have -- from "How do I get a CENOMAR?" to "What is a reasonable per-head catering rate in Tagaytay?" 5. **Dream-Forward Brand Identity.** In a competitive landscape of dated directories, bride-focused blogs, and pink luxury brands, haraya's ethereal lavender-and-blue identity stands alone. The name itself -- a Filipino word meaning "imagination" and "dream" -- creates an emotional connection that English brand names cannot match.

**For Filipino couples** planning a celebration, **haraya** is the **celebration planning platform** that turns their dreams into a plan they can actually follow -- **unlike** scattered Facebook groups, outdated directories, and bride-focused blogs. **haraya** combines a curated vendor directory, intelligent planning tools, an AI assistant named Kasa, and a distinctly Filipino identity built on Dream Lavender and Twilight Blue -- **where every dream and every celebration is welcome.**

## 10. BRAND RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

These rules apply to ALL code, UI copy, marketing materials, social media content, vendor communications, email templates, documentation, and internal communications. No exceptions.

### Rule 1: Always "haraya" in Lowercase

The brand name is always written as `haraya` -- one word, all lowercase, no exceptions.

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| haraya | Haraya |
| haraya | HARAYA |
| haraya | HaRaYa |
| haraya | Hara Ya |
| haraya | hara-ya |

In contexts where capitalization is forced by the platform (e.g., the start of a sentence in a social media post where lowercase may look like a typo), the preferred approach is to restructure the sentence so that "haraya" does not appear at the start. If unavoidable, use lowercase anyway and let the brand consistency speak for itself.

### Rule 2: Never Assume Gender

haraya is built for every couple. The platform never assumes the gender, orientation, or relationship structure of its users.

| Instead Of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Bride / Groom | "You" and "Your Partner" (default) |
| Bride's checklist | Your checklist |
| Bridal party | Wedding party / Your crew |
| Maid of Honor / Best Man | (Customizable role names) |
| Mr. and Mrs. | (Couple's chosen title) |
| Bride and groom | The couple / The partners |
| Bridesmaids / Groomsmen | Attendants / Wedding party |
| His and hers | Theirs / Both partners |
| Wedding | Celebration (where appropriate and natural) |

**Default partner labels:** "You" and "Your Partner" in all UI contexts. Couples can customize to their names, "Bride/Groom" (if they want traditional terms), or any custom text. The platform never forces terminology on anyone.

**The `CoupleProfile` type:** Uses `partner1Name` and `partner2Name`. Never `brideName` and `groomName`.

### Rule 3: "All Celebrations Welcome" Badge

Vendors can opt in to display the "All Celebrations Welcome" badge on their profile, signaling that they welcome all couples regardless of gender, orientation, or ceremony type.

- Badge background: Dream Lavender (#9D8BD8)
- Badge text: Pearl (#FDFCFE), Poppins 500, uppercase, 0.05em letter-spacing
- Badge is a boolean (`allCelebrationsWelcome`) on the Vendor type
- Displayed on vendor cards and vendor profile pages
- Badge is opt-in only -- vendors are never required or pressured to display it
- The badge is NOT the centerpiece of the brand. It is a signal that some couples look for and others simply do not notice.

### Rule 4: Ceremony Types Must Be Inclusive

The platform supports the following ceremony types, and this list must appear in full wherever ceremony type selection is offered:

- Catholic
- Church (INC)
- Church (Other)
- Muslim
- Civil
- Civil Union
- Other

**Never default to Catholic.** Always let the user choose. If no choice is made, the system should either prompt for a selection or provide a generic (non-ceremony-specific) experience.

In the Checklist and Budget tools, ceremony type filters the visible items. Catholic-specific items (Pre-Cana, arrhae, church fees) appear only when Catholic is selected. Muslim-specific items (nikah, mahr) appear only when Muslim is selected. Civil and Civil Union share most items. "Other" shows all items so the couple can choose what applies.

### Rule 5: Filipino-Specific Terminology

The following Filipino terms MUST be used correctly throughout the platform. They are part of haraya's cultural intelligence and should never be replaced with English approximations.

| Term | Definition | Context |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **CENOMAR** | Certificate of No Marriage, issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) | Documentation checklist, Kasa guidance |
| **Pre-Cana** | Pre-marriage counseling seminar required by the Catholic Church | Catholic ceremony checklist, Kasa guidance |
| **Ninong / Ninang** | Principal sponsors (godfather/godmother) in a Filipino wedding | Checklist items, entourage management |
| **Arrhae** | 13 wedding coins used in Catholic Filipino wedding ceremonies | Catholic ceremony checklist |
| **Lechon** | Roast pig, a staple at Filipino celebrations | Catering vendor context, budget categories |
| **Mahr** | Dowry or gift from the groom to the bride in Muslim weddings | Muslim ceremony checklist |
| **Nikah** | The Muslim marriage ceremony | Muslim ceremony context |
| **Barong Tagalog** | Traditional Filipino formal wear for men | Attire vendor context |
| **Filipiniana** | Traditional Filipino formal attire (especially for women) | Attire vendor context |
| **Same-Day Edit (SDE)** | A video highlight of the wedding shown during the reception, edited in real-time | Photo & Video vendor context |
| **Cord / Veil / Arrhae sponsors** | Secondary sponsors who participate in the Catholic ceremony | Catholic ceremony checklist |
| **Tita / Tito** | Aunt / Uncle -- used broadly for older family friends | Content voice, relatable copy |
| **Pamanhikan** | A traditional Filipino custom where the groom's family visits the bride's family to formally ask for her hand | Checklist, cultural content |

### Rule 6: Currency Is Always PHP

All monetary values on the platform are in Philippine Pesos (PHP).

- Format: `PHP X,XXX` with the PHP prefix, or `X,XXX` with the peso sign
- Use `toLocaleString('en-PH')` for number formatting in code

- Per-head catering: Display as "450 - 800 per head"
- Never display prices in USD, EUR, or any other currency on the Philippine platform

- Body: Poppins (never Jost, which is retired)

### Rule 11: The Woven Heritage System Is Retired

The following elements from the previous brand identity are fully retired and must not appear in any haraya context:

- The six-textile color mapping (Pina, T'nalak, Inabel, Hablon, Yakan, Langkit)
- The woven border (6px multicolor horizontal stripe)
- Textile background patterns (.pattern-pina, .pattern-inabel, etc.)
- Section-to-textile color rules
- The Dropped-E signature (forevr, discovr, togethr, remembr)
- Heritage Red (#8B3A3A) as a brand color
- The Jost + Cormorant Garamond type system
- Warm ivory/cream backgrounds (#FDFAF3, #F5EDD8)
- The e-a monogram logo

These elements may persist in the codebase during the transition period but must be systematically replaced. No new code, content, or design should reference them.

### Rule 12: Kasa Is the AI Assistant

The AI assistant is named "Kasa" (not "Ate AI," not "Tulay," not "the AI assistant" in user-facing copy).

- Always "Kasa" with a capital K (it is a proper name, unlike the brand name "haraya")
- In product UI: "Ask Kasa" / "Kasa suggests..." / "Chat with Kasa"
- In technical documentation: "Kasa (AI assistant)" for clarity
- Voice: warm, knowledgeable, gently witty, uses Filipino terms naturally
- Never robotic or corporate in tone

### Rule 13: Photography and Imagery

All photography used in haraya materials must:
- Feature real Filipino people and real Philippine settings
- Include diversity in couple configurations, ceremony types, skin tones, body types, and celebration styles
- Feel warm, dreamy, and authentic -- not overly staged or clinical
- Never use exclusively bride-in-white imagery as the default representation of celebration
- Credit photographers and source materials appropriately

### Rule 14: Accessibility

- All text must meet WCAG AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text)
- Color must never be the sole indicator of state (important for the 8% of males with color vision deficiency)
- All interactive elements must be keyboard accessible
- All images must have meaningful alt text
- Badge font sizes must be at least 11px
- Touch targets on mobile must be at least 44x44 pixels
