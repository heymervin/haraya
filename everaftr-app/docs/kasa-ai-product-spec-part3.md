- **Candle Ceremony:** Each of you holds a candle and together lights a unity candle.

**Kasa:** Absolutely. While same-sex marriage and civil unions aren't legally recognized in the Philippines yet, there are important legal instruments you can put in place to protect your partnership:

**Available Legal Protections:**

1. **Special Power of Attorney (SPA)** -- Allows each of you to make medical and legal decisions for the other. Available in Quezon City and San Juan City through the "Right to Care" program. PHP 3,000-5,000 through a lawyer.

2. **Co-ownership Agreements** -- If you own property together, have a lawyer draft a co-ownership agreement. Protects both of your interests.

3. **Wills and Estate Planning** -- Since you won't automatically inherit from each other under current law, having proper wills is important.

4. **Insurance Beneficiary Designation** -- Name each other as beneficiaries on life insurance policies.

5. **Joint Bank Accounts** -- Most banks allow joint accounts regardless of marital status.

I'd recommend consulting with a lawyer who specializes in LGBTQ+ rights. Budget around PHP 10,000-20,000 for the legal paperwork. It's worth it for peace of mind.
