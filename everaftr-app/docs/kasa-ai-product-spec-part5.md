## 5.5 Document Requirements by Ceremony Type

### Catholic Wedding Documents

| Document | Where to Get | Processing Time | Validity | When to Start | Notes |
|----------|-------------|----------------|----------|---------------|-------|
| PSA CENOMAR | PSA Serbilis / psahelpline.ph | Walk-in: 1-2 days. Online: 3-10 days (MM), 10-15 days (province) | 6 months | 6-10 months before | Start early. Name discrepancies cause delays. |
| PSA Birth Certificate | PSA Serbilis / psahelpline.ph | Walk-in: same day. Online: 3-10 days | No expiry | 6-10 months before | Verify name matches all other documents. |
| Baptismal Certificate (FOR MARRIAGE PURPOSES) | Parish where baptized | 1-3 weeks (longer if different province) | 6 months | 6-10 months before | Must have "FOR MARRIAGE PURPOSES" annotation. |
| Confirmation Certificate | Parish where confirmed | 1-3 weeks | 6 months | 6-10 months before | If not confirmed, you may need to receive the sacrament first. |
| Pre-Cana Seminar Certificate | Parish or accredited organization (CEE, CEFAM, DW) | Same day after attending | Varies by parish | 6-8 months before | Both partners must attend together. |
| Canonical Interview Clearance | Parish where marrying | Same day | Varies | 4-8 months before | Priest interviews each partner separately. |
| Marriage License | Local Civil Registrar (LCR) | 10-day posting + 2-3 days processing = ~2-3 weeks | 120 days (4 months) | 2-4 months before | CRITICAL: Time this so it's valid on wedding day but not applied too early. |
| Cedula / Community Tax Certificate | City/Municipal Hall | Same day | 1 year (Jan-Dec) | 1-3 months before | Cheap and easy to get. |
| Permit for Mixed Marriage | Diocese | 2-4 weeks | Varies | 4-6 months before | Only if one partner is non-Catholic. |
| Death Certificate of Spouse | PSA | 1-2 weeks | No expiry | As early as possible | Only if widowed. |
| Annulment Decree | Court | Should already be final | No expiry | Must be done before planning | Only if previously married. Must be final and registered. |

## 5.7 Peak Season and Timing Data

### Philippine Wedding Seasons

| Period | Season | Demand | Price Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|-------------|-------|
| January | Post-holiday | Medium | Standard | Good time to start planning for year-end weddings. |
| February | Valentine's season | Very High | +15-30% | Feb 14 and surrounding weekends are extremely popular. |
| March | Dry season begins | High | +10-20% | Holy Week: most churches DO NOT allow weddings. |
| April | Dry season | High | +10-20% | Summer weddings popular. School break = easier for guests. |
| May | End of dry season | High | +10-20% | Flores de Mayo. Last chance before rainy season. |
| June | Rainy season begins | Medium-Low | -10-20% | Discounts available. Need rain backup plans. |
| July | Rainy season | Low | -10-30% | Best time for budget weddings. Fewer bookings = more availability. |
| August | Rainy season | Low | -10-30% | Off-peak discounts. Ghost month (Chinese belief) = some couples avoid. |
| September | Ber months begin | Medium | Standard | Demand starts picking up as year-end approaches. |
| October | Ber months | High | +10-15% | Popular month. Not as expensive as Dec-Feb. |
| November | Ber months | High | +10-15% | Very popular. All Saints'/Souls' Day week may have limited church schedules. |
| December | Peak season | Very High | +20-40% | THE most popular wedding month. Book venue 12-18 months ahead. |
